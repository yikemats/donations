Mask vs. Zuckerberg:
Billionaire Showdown! Who Gives Back More to Society?

July 23, 2023

On June 24th, a text message arrived on my smartphone from a friend living in Los Angeles. It read, "If you have any means to stop them from fighting, I hope you'll do everything in your power to do so." Curious, I opened the link that accompanied the message and found an article with the following title:

"UFC(Ultimate Fighting Championship) president Dana White said he talked to Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg on the phone for 1.5 hours — and that both are 'dead serious' about fighting"

At first, I thought it was just a joke. My friend often sends humorous video clips for laughs. However, as time went on, even major media outlets in the US started covering articles with an increasingly serious tone, suggesting that the fight might actually happen.

White confirmed that he had a 1.5-hour phone call with both Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg regarding the matter. According to his comments, both of them showed a positive attitude towards the fight and agreed to donate a portion of the revenue to charity. Las Vegas appears to be the most likely location for the event.

Let It Be A Meaningful Battle

While it would certainly be fascinating to witness such a battle, both individuals are CEOs of major tech companies, and any unforeseen circumstances could disrupt their businesses. As per the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, Musk's net worth was $232 billion, ranking first, and Zuckerberg's was $107 billion, ranking ninth as of July 23rd. It is difficult to imagine why either of them would physically fight, especially considering the potential risks to their companies. However, two plausible reasons could be "promotional benefits for their businesses" and "charitable contributions."

Zuckerberg is known for actively engaging in charitable activities related to healthcare, among other causes, alongside his wife, Priscilla Chan, who is a Chinese-American pediatrician. On the other hand, Elon Musk's charitable efforts are not as widely recognized. If they were to fight, perhaps they could compete based on the "amount of charitable donations." Using ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer, I scraped XML tax documents released for 2020 and 2021, to calculate the total amount of donations made by the charitable organizations they run.

So, who would win?

Thanks to his wife's philanthropic endeavors, Mark Zuckerberg emerged as the winner.

According to the tax documents, Zuckerberg has three organizations - "Chan Zuckerberg Initiative Advocacy," "Chan Zuckerberg Initiative Foundation," and "Chan Zuckerberg Biohub" - through which he provides donations systematically by employing staff to handle the process. In contrast, Elon Musk has only one organization, the "Musk Foundation," which is managed by him and another individual known for handling personal assets, JARED BIRCHALL.

The areas to which they donated money provide insight into their respective characters. Zuckerberg's donations were directed towards healthcare, racial equality efforts, and other causes, possibly influenced by his wife.

On the other hand, Musk's donations were primarily directed towards funding technology-related contests, university research, and children's healthcare. Musk tragically lost his first child a few weeks after birth, which may have influenced these choices.

In any case, both individuals enjoy being in the limelight. If the money raised from the fight benefits society in some way, it might not be merely a self-indulgent endeavor after all.
